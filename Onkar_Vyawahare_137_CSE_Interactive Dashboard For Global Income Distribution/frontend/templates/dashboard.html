Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Total Countries The total number of unique nations currently tracked and analyzed within the primary dataset.
Total GDP Per Capita Aggregated average of the economic output per person, adjusted for purchasing power parity.
Total Gini Index A statistical measure representing the income distribution of a nation's residents; used to gauge inequality.
Unemployment Rate Percentage of the total labor force that is without work but actively seeking employment.
Country Strength Score A proprietary weighted index that balances GDP growth against social stability and labor healthy.
Inequality Severity Qualitative assessment of the risk posed by wealth dispersion to long-term economic sustainability.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What does the "Country Strength Score" represent?
The Country Strength Score is a composite index calculated by weighing absolute wealth (GDP Per Capita) against structural stability indicators like the Gini Index and Unemployment rate. A higher score indicates a resilient economy with relatively balanced wealth distribution.
2. How is "Income Inequality Severity" determined?
Inequality Severity uses the Gini Index (0-100 scale) as a base. We categorize scores above 50 as 'Extreme Disparity', 40-50 as 'Moderate Dispersion', and below 35 as 'Relatively Balanced'. The dashboard aggregates this score to highlight regions requiring structural intervention.
3. What is the source of this economic data?
The current dataset is derived from the Global Income Distribution Dataset.xlsx, which mirrors World Bank and IMF economic indicators spanning 2001-2024. It covers 20 major nations analyzed for wealth and labor trends.
4. Why do some countries show 0% income share for the lowest 20%?
A 0% income share usually denotes a data reporting gap or a lack of granular survey data for that specific reporting year in the primary dataset. Our analysis treats these as null identifiers during aggregation to maintain accuracy.
