Project Overview

The Global Income Distribution Analytics (GIDA) project is a comprehensive data analysis platform designed to uncover actionable insights into global economic disparities. Through the collection and processing of international datasets, this project provides an interactive lens through which researchers and policymakers can observe the nuanced interplay between wealth generation, inequality, and labor market resilience. Our mission is to transform raw statistical records into accessible, interactive narratives that foster a deeper understanding of macro-economic structures worldwide.

Detailed Inequality Analysis

GDP Per Capita

Definition: Gross Domestic Product divided by the population. It represents the average economic output per person.

Unit: US Dollars ($)

Significance: Acts as the baseline indicator of national wealth and standard of living, though it does not account for how wealth is distributed among citizens.

Gini Index

Definition: A statistical measure of economic inequality in a population. It quantifies the disparity between the richest and poorest.

Unit: Index Score (0 to 100)

Significance: 0 represents perfect equality, while 100 represents maximal inequality. It specifically answers the true question of structural wealth distribution.

Income Share

Definition: The percentage of total national income held by specific segments of the population.

Unit: Percentage (%)

Brackets Overview:
- Lowest 20%: The poorest quintile.
- Second 20%: Lower-middle class.
- Third 20%: The middle class.
- Fourth 20%: Upper-middle class.
- Highest 20%: The wealthiest quintile.

Unemployment Rate

Definition: The percentage of the labor force that is jobless and actively looking for work.

Unit: Percentage (%)

Significance: Acts as a multiplier to inequality. Chronic gaps in labor market integration prevent broad social mobility even in increasingly wealthy nations.

Global Insights

These insights are derived directly from the Global Income Distribution Dataset.xlsx, spanning 2001 to 2024 across 20 distinct nations.

  • Polarization of Wealth The data reveals a squeeze on the middle and lower classes across the surveyed nations, highlighting a distinct shift of wealth towards the highest income quintile.
  • Consistent High Inequality South Africa consistently displays the highest inequality, with a peak Gini score of 73.0 within the dataset, demonstrating severe structural disparity.
  • Global Labor Disconnect Despite economic growth (rising GDP), an average unemployment rate of ~7.97% across the dataset indicates the labor market's failure to fully integrate populations, acting as a direct driver of inequality.
